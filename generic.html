Please read on to get to know a little more about me.
Thank you for your time!

After obtaining my bachelors's degree in Spanish, I started my career in the tech world in Customer Support. I joined an EDI company, advising customers on data transmission errors and best practices, and troubleshooting connections to retailers and setups between suppliers and retailers. It was in this role that I found my passion for development and data. I then moved into a specialized role that provided analysis of data trends and errors to help customers reduce their mistakes and help them become more savvy in their creation of documents. During this time, I partnered with technology teams on a project to make a major security transition in communication methods. From there, I transitioned to a development team as a Quality Engineer. After about a year of testing Java code that transformed data to a standard format for transmission, I was assigned to a project for retailer onboarding for our data analytics platform.

During this project, I sharpened my programming skills in Python and C# and utilized SQL extensively to update databases. I also became even more familiar with the Software Development Life Cycle, and was part of weekly releases. It was during this time that I realized that I wanted to explore data analysis further. I took some time to try a graduate program in Linguistics, but found that it was not for me. During the pandemic, I returned to Wisconsin, where I started a Paralegal/Legal Studies program. I then moved to New York, where I have been continuing the program remotely. I also realized that Data Science is a passion of mine, and I have been working on a boot camp where I have been using real world problems to explore data analysis, machine learning, and language learning through Python and SQL. I am in the process of completing an externship as well. My goal is to obtain work in Data Science in the future.
